Idle Sambar

Description

Idli Sambhar is a popular South Indian dish consisting of soft, steamed rice cakes (idli) paired with a flavorful, tangy lentil soup (sambhar). Idli is made from fermented rice and urad dal (black gram) batter, which is steamed into light, fluffy cakes. It is often served with sambhar, a spicy and aromatic stew made from toor dal (pigeon peas), vegetables, tamarind, and a blend of spices, giving it a unique balance of tangy and spicy flavors.

This combination is not only delicious but also nutritious, as it provides a good balance of carbohydrates, proteins, and fiber. It is commonly enjoyed for breakfast or as a light meal across India, particularly in southern states.

Ingredients

For Sambar Masala Paste

  • 2 tbsp dried/desiccated coconut
  • 2 tbsp chana dal/ split desi chickpeas
  • 2 tbsp urad dal/ split matpe beans
  • 1 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 1 tsp jeera/ cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp whole black peppercorns
  • 1 tsp menthya seeds/ methi seeds/ Fenugreek seeds
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 1/4 inch piece chakke/cinnamon bark
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 3 dried red chilis
  • 1 drop oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion (roughly chopped)
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric

For Sambhar

  • 1/2 cup dry toor dal/ split pigeon peas
  • tbsp ghee total
  • 2 medium yellow onions (thinly sliced)
  • 1 cup pearl onions
  • 2 medium tomatoes (finely chopped)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp jaggery

Steps

Prep the Sambar Masala Paste

  1. Heat a small frying pan at a medium flame. Don't add oil.
  2. Once the pan becomes hot, add 2 tbsp dried coconut. Roast till the dried coconut becomes slightly golden brown. Then, take off flame and transfer to a plate.
  3. Add 2 tbsp chana dal to the pan and roast for a few seconds.
  4. Add 2 tbsp urad dal and roast for a few seconds.
  5. Add 1 tbsp coriander ceeds and roast for a few seconds.
  6. Add 1 tsp jeera/ cumin seeds and roast for a few seconds.
  7. Add 1 tsp black pepper and roast for a few seconds.
  8. Add 1 tsp menthya seeds/ Fenugreek seeds and roast for a few seconds.
  9. Add 1 tbsp sesame seeds and roast for a few seconds.
  10. Add 1 small piece of chakke/ cinnamon bark and 2-3 cloves. Roast for a few seconds.
  11. Finally, break 3 dried red chilis and add to the pan. Roast for a few more seconds.
  12. Take everything off flame and transfer to the plate that the coconut is cooling on. Let cool for a few minutes.
  13. Transfer spices to a small blender/food processor and dry grind to a fine masala powder.
  14. Then, in the same small frying pan that you roasted the spices, add a drop of oil. Add 1 medium onion (roughly chopped) and roast till the onion becomes golden brown. Transfer to the blender.
  15. Also add about 1/2 cup water and 1/2 tsp turmeric to the blender. Grind to a wet masala paste. Keep aside.

Make the Sambar

  1. Cook 1/2 cup of toor dal with 3 cups of water in a pressure cooker at a medium flame for 2 whistles.
  2. After 2 whistles, turn off the flame and release the pressure. Remove the lid.
  3. Keep the pressure cooker on the stove and heat it up at a medium flame once again.
  4. If the dal looks too thick, add more water. Let the toor dal come to a slow boil. Then, turn down the flame to a low flame and let it continue to simmer.
  5. Meanwhile, in a separate frying pan, heat 2 tbsp ghee at a medium flame.
  6. Add 2 medium yellow onions (thinly sliced) and 1 cup pearl onions. Sauté till the onions are golden brown. Add the browned onions to the boiling toor dal. (Note: onions nicely browned in ghee is crucial for lingering Sambar!)
  7. In the same pan, add another 2 tbsp ghee.
  8. Add 2 medium tomatoes (finely chopped). Sauté for a few minutes. The tomatoes should become nice and mushy. Then, add to the boiling toor dal.
  9. Now, add the masala paste and mix well.
  10. Finally, add salt to taste.
  11. Let the sambar simmer for a few more minutes.
  12. Once the sambar is done cooking, add 1 tbsp of jaggery. Mix well and take off flame. Enjoy with hot Idlis!
